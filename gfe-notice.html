No Surprises Act

Your right to a Good Faith Estimate.

This notice describes your rights to receive a Good Faith Estimate (GFE) for the cost of mental health care services if you are uninsured or choose self-pay.

Download the notice (PDF) View fees & insurance
About GFEs

Who receives a Good Faith Estimate?

If you are uninsured or choose to pay out of pocket (self-pay) for services, you are entitled to a Good Faith Estimate that details the expected costs of your care. This applies to counseling, EMDR intensives, and coaching services offered by Teresa R. Foster, L.C.S.W.

What’s included

Information you will receive

  • Itemized list of anticipated services and fees
  • Diagnosis codes (if applicable) and CPT codes
  • Estimated total cost of services for a 12-month period
  • Instructions for requesting updates or additional estimates
Timeline

When you will receive your estimate

You will receive a written Good Faith Estimate:

  • Within 1 business day of scheduling a service if the appointment is at least 3 business days away
  • Within 3 business days of scheduling if the appointment is at least 10 business days away
  • Within 3 business days of request if you have not yet scheduled
Disputing a bill

If your bill is higher than your estimate

If you receive a bill that is at least $400 more than your Good Faith Estimate, you can dispute the bill. Keep a copy of your estimate. You may contact Teresa R. Foster, L.C.S.W. to review the charges, and you can initiate a dispute through the U.S. Department of Health and Human Services. For more information, visit cms.gov/nosurprises or call (800) 985-3059.
